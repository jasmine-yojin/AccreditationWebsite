Substantial Equivalence

In order to meet state's comulsory education requirements, students and parents must show that the home school education program is equivalent to public high school education. This is called the substantial equivalence. In order to receive the letter of substantial equivalence, the student must register with the state from the point they start the home school. That means unregistered students who have received education in non-accredited schools or online schools need to sit for GED/HSE. Therefore, you should not direct every homeschool student to receive a letter from the district because the situatuions may be different. Please note that the letter must come from public school district.

Residency Matters

If a student indicated that they were "homeschooled" on their application and was homeschooled virtually, we will only accept based on the state of their residency. i.e., the student was virutally homeschooled in California and the student lives in NYC. If the student provides a superintendent letter from NYS, we will accept it.

New York State

If not graduated yet, the homeschooled students will need to send two letters from the superintendent: one during senior year and the final one that states that they graduated/met requirements for graduation. If graduated, only the final letter is required. The NYS superintendent letter will not straightforwardly say that the home school program is equivalent to public school education, however, it is a letter of substantial equivalence. Acceptable home school letter examples are provided below.

Out-Of-State

Not every state issues home school verification letter to home school students. Out-of-state home school verification letters from the state's district are similar to that of New York. Be sure to look for the language of substantial equivalence in the letter. If the letter from the state is present, that letter serves as the diploma. Some states issue actual state diploma for home schooled students. If the student cannot provide a letter or a state-issued diploma, they must sit for the GED or High School Equivalency Exam. Acceptable Out-of-State Letter Examples

Ohio Homeschool Letter

"...completed his required courseork for the State of Ohio..." implies substantial equivalence.

Maryland Homeschool Letter

Substantial equivalence is stated by "regular, thorough instruction in the subjects required by the Maryland Code of Regulations..."

Illinois Homeschool Letter

"...met the Illinois requirements..." show substantial equivalence.

Florida Homeschool Letter

"...was enrolled as a home schooled student in Duval County Public Schools..."

North Carolina Homeschool Letter

"...full and appropirate evidence of academic progress and completion of high school coursework..."

Florida Homeschool Letter

"Orange County Public Schools' Home Education Program under Florida Statute 1002.4..."

Unacceptable NY Homeschool Letter Examples

This is an example of Individualized Home Instruction Plan. However, we do not accept this as a proof of homeschool education.

Letter from School Staff

This letter is written by an administrator of the homeschool program that parents utilized to educate their child. The letter has to come from the public school district.

Affidavit Signed By Parents

Several states accept affidavit of homeschool signed by parents. However, CUNY adheres to the NYS practice and require a letter from the district.
